Every movement here addresses forward head posture, anterior pelvic tilt, or weak core — the three patterns your body currently carries. Quality over quantity.
Morning Posture Reset
5 minutes · every day
Wall Stand
2 min
  1. Stand with back of head, shoulder blades, buttocks, and heels against the wall
  2. Keep chin level — do not tilt head up or down
  3. Gap between lower back and wall: should fit a flat palm, not a fist
  4. Breathe slowly; feel the new baseline
Cat–Cow
8 reps · slow
  1. On hands and knees, wrists under shoulders, knees under hips
  2. Inhale: arch back gently, lift chest and tailbone (cow)
  3. Exhale: round back up, tuck chin and pelvis (cat)
  4. Move with breath, not momentum
Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
30s each side
  1. Kneel on one knee (cushion under it), other foot flat in front, 90° angle
  2. Squeeze glutes of the back-leg side — tuck tailbone under
  3. Shift hips gently forward, not by leaning — by glute contraction
  4. Feel stretch in the front of the hip of the kneeling leg
Do not arch lower back. The stretch comes from glute squeeze + forward hip, not spine bending.
True Hip Flexor Stretch — Mike Reinold
Training A · Lower + Core
20 min · Mon / Wed (alternate with B)
Glute Bridge
3 × 15
  1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat, hip-width apart
  2. Press heels into floor, squeeze glutes, lift hips until knees–hips–shoulders form a line
  3. Hold 2 seconds at top, lower slowly
  4. Key: the lift comes from glutes, not lower back
Glute Bridge — Form Tutorial
Dead Bug
3 × 10/side
  1. Lie on back, arms reaching up, knees bent 90°, shins parallel to floor
  2. Exhale: slowly lower opposite arm and leg toward the floor
  3. Keep lower back pressed to the floor the entire time
  4. Return to start, switch sides
If your back arches off the floor, reduce the range. Control > range.
Dead Bug — NASM Official
Bulgarian Split Squat
3 × 10/side
  1. Rear foot elevated on a chair or sofa behind you
  2. Front foot far enough forward that knee stays over ankle when bending
  3. Lower straight down, front thigh parallel to floor
  4. Push through front heel to rise
Side Plank
2 × 30s/side
  1. Lie on side, forearm on floor, elbow under shoulder
  2. Lift hips until body forms a straight line from head to feet
  3. Gentler on neck than front plank — good for straight-neck issues
Training B · Upper + Posture
20 min · alternates with A
Doorframe Chest Stretch
2 × 30s
  1. Stand in doorway, place forearms on the frame at shoulder height
  2. Step one foot through, let chest fall forward gently
  3. Feel the stretch across chest and front shoulders
  4. Crucial for fixing rounded shoulders + forward head
Knee Push-up
3 × 8–10
  1. Knees on mat, hands slightly wider than shoulders
  2. Body forms straight line from knees to head — no sagging hips
  3. Lower chest to almost touch the floor, push back up
  4. Elbows angle ~45° from body, not flared wide
Band Row
3 × 12
  1. Anchor band at chest height (door, railing)
  2. Stand tall, pull handles toward ribs, elbows back and down
  3. Squeeze shoulder blades together at the end
  4. This is the direct antidote to your hunched posture
Bird Dog
3 × 10/side
  1. Hands and knees, neutral spine
  2. Extend opposite arm and leg simultaneously — slow and stable
  3. Hold 2 seconds, return without rushing
  4. Hips stay level — no rotation
Chin Tuck
3 × 10
  1. Stand or sit tall — imagine a string pulling crown upward
  2. Slide chin straight back, not down (not a nod)
  3. A small double chin appearing = correct form
  4. Hold 3 seconds, release
If it becomes "looking down," you're doing it wrong. The motion is horizontal, like pulling your head back on a track.
Chin Tuck — Dr. Oliver
Training C · Full Body
20 min · once weekly
Wall Angel
3 × 10
  1. Back against wall: head, upper back, buttocks touching
  2. Arms in "W" shape against wall, elbows bent 90°
  3. Slide arms up to "Y" keeping contact with wall throughout
  4. Most people cannot fully touch at first — that's the point
Bodyweight Squat
3 × 15
  1. Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
  2. Sit back and down — knees track over middle toes
  3. Thighs toward parallel, chest stays lifted
  4. Drive through heels to stand
Dumbbell Deadlift (Light)
3 × 12
  1. Start with 3kg dumbbells in each hand
  2. Hinge at hips — push butt back, chest forward, back flat
  3. Lower until you feel hamstring stretch (no need to touch floor)
  4. Squeeze glutes to stand
Keep neck aligned with spine — do not look up.
Single-leg Glute Bridge
2 × 10/side
  1. Set up like regular bridge, extend one leg straight
  2. Lift hips using the planted leg only
  3. Hips stay level — no dropping on one side
  4. Advanced version — do when regular bridge feels easy
Swimming Strategy
45 min · twice weekly
  1. Warm-up: slow breaststroke 200m
  2. Main: backstroke 100m × 4 (the posture-correcting stroke)
  3. Main: breaststroke 200m × 2
  4. Cool-down: slow breaststroke 100m
Avoid freestyle — the side-breathing aggravates straight-neck. Backstroke opens the chest, reverses forward head posture.
